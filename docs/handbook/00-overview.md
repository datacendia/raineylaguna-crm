# 00 — Overview: how it all fits together

**Who this is for:** everyone. Read this once to understand the whole picture.

---

## Two systems, one goal

| System | Address | What it does |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Marketing site** | `raineylaguna.com` | Where prospects find us. It explains the services, and lets a business run a **free instant audit** of their own website. Some visitors fill in a contact form or message us — they become leads. |
| **CRM** | `crm.raineylaguna.com` | Where **we** work. It finds prospects, scores them, drafts and sends outreach, and tracks every conversation from first contact to closed deal. |

The two are connected: when someone uses the audit or contact form on the site,
their details (and their audit result) are sent straight into the CRM as a new
lead.

## The lead lifecycle

Every business moves through the same stages (you'll see these on the **Pipeline**):

1. **Lead** — in the system, not yet contacted. Either we discovered them, or they
   came to us through the site.
2. **Contacted** — we've sent a first message.
3. **Audited** — we've shared a digital audit / proposal of what we'd improve.
4. **Proposal** — a concrete offer is out.
5. **Closed** — won (a client!) or set aside.

Your daily job (see [02 — Daily Workflow](02-daily-workflow.md)) is to move
businesses along this path, a few at a time, every day.

## Where leads come from

- **Discovery** — the CRM can find businesses automatically (from Google Maps or
  the free OpenStreetMap database) and add them as leads.
- **The website** — people who run the audit or fill the contact form arrive
  automatically as leads, already tagged with their source and audit score.
- **You** — referrals, events, or a CSV import.

## Glossary

| Term | Plain meaning |
|------|---------------|
| **Lead** | A business we might work with. |
| **Pipeline / stage** | Where a lead is on the journey from first contact to client. |
| **Audit** | A health-check of a business's website — a 0–100 score plus a list of concrete problems. Our opening hook. |
| **Draft** | A message the CRM has written for you to review before sending. |
| **Outreach** | A message that has actually been sent, and its result. |
| **Digest** | The weekly Monday summary of activity. |
| **Market / city** | Which place a lead belongs to. It decides the language we write in and which channels we're allowed to use. |
| **Potential / score** | How promising a lead looks, to help you prioritise. |
